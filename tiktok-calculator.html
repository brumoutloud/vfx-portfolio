Understanding TikTok Monetization

Creator Fund vs. Creativity Program

TikTok has replaced the old **Creator Fund** with the new **Creativity Program**. Here's the key difference:

  • Creator Fund (Old System): This program paid creators from a large, but *fixed*, pot of money each day. As more videos went viral, that fixed amount had to be split between more and more creators, which often led to very low and unpredictable payouts. It's now been phased out in most regions.
  • Creativity Program (New System): This is a performance-based model with no fixed daily fund. It's designed to offer higher potential earnings by paying a rate (RPM) for every 1,000 qualified views on videos that are **over one minute long**. Your earnings are directly tied to your performance, not how many other people had a viral video that day. This calculator is based on this new program.
Am I eligible for the program? ↓

To join the TikTok Creativity Program, you generally need to meet these criteria:

  • Be at least 18 years old.
  • Have at least 10,000 followers.
  • Have at least 100,000 video views in the last 30 days.
  • Post original content that is over 1 minute long.
What are RPM & Qualified Views? ↓
Qualified Views:

A "qualified view" is one that TikTok deems valuable enough to pay for. It must be watched for at least 5 seconds, come from the 'For You' page, and not be a repeat view. Only a percentage of your total views count towards earnings.

RPM (Revenue Per Mille):

This stands for Revenue Per 1,000 qualified views. The rate changes based on factors like audience location (US/UK is higher), video topic, and total watch time.
